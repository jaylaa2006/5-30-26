## Gaps — not freely available as full PDF

- **Ebbell, *The Papyrus Ebers* (1937)** — Bendix Ebbell's English translation. Not freely downloadable from archive.org or HathiTrust; archive.org listings (`papyrusebers0000cyri`, `ancientegyptianm0000unse_w1u0`) are borrow-only and the latter is actually a Bryan reprint. Copyright status varies by jurisdiction (Ebbell d. 1968 → still under life+70 in EU/UK until 2039). Options:
  - Borrow via archive.org's controlled digital lending
  - Order print or scan from a research library (HathiTrust catalog: 001231932)
  - Use the Wreszinski 1913 hieroglyphic transcript here + secondary scholarship in the meantime
- **Bryan, *The Papyrus Ebers* (1930)** — Cyril P. Bryan's English interpretation. Same situation: archive.org `papyrusebers0000cyri` is borrow-only. The widely-circulated `ask-force.org` and `bxscience.edu` PDFs are short excerpts (9–22 pages), not the full 200+ page translation. HathiTrust full view is access-restricted. Options:
  - Borrow via archive.org
  - HathiTrust catalog: `coo.31924073200077`

## Citation hygiene

When citing Ebers remedies in Plants of Power cards, use Ebbell's numbering (e.g., "Ebers 261" for garlic). Since Ebbell isn't included here yet, verify against the Wreszinski 1913 hieroglyphic transcript + secondary literature until the Ebbell PDF is sourced.
