# Theming it — make it look and sound like yours

## Colors
Open `maat-reader.html` and find the `:root{` block near the top (around line
97). Those are the color tokens the whole app uses:
```css
:root{
  --gold:#D4A843; --lapis:#1B3A65; --carnelian:#CC4422;
  --malachite:#2E7D32; --sandstone:#F2E4CC; ...
}
```
Change the hex values to your palette. Tell Claude Code:
> "Change the :root color tokens in maat-reader.html to a palette of deep teal,
> coral, and warm cream, and update any hardcoded matching colors."

## Name & branding
- **App title / tab name:** the `<title>` and the `<meta>` tags at the top of `maat-reader.html`.
- **App name on phones:** `manifest.json` (`name`, `short_name`).
- **Landing page text:** `index.html` and the intro/landing section of `maat-reader.html`
  (e.g. the "Enter the House of Life" headline).
- **Icon/favicon:** replace `favicon.ico`, `favicon-32x32.png`, `apple-touch-icon.png`, `icon.svg`.

A few in-app labels from the original theme (words like "Per Ankh", "Maat",
"Kemet", "House of Life") still appear in the interface. Ask Claude Code:
> "Find every user-visible 'Per Ankh' / 'Maat' / 'Kemet' / 'House of Life' label in
> maat-reader.html and replace them with my theme's wording, but leave the example
> story text alone."

## The guide character
The app has a teacher/helper character (originally "Seba Khafre") who appears on
the landing and introduces stories. Two parts to re-theme:

1. **Art** — the portraits live in `art/seba-khafre/` (files like `teaching.png`).
   **Replace these images with your own character, keeping the same filenames** so
   the app finds them. (If you delete them without replacing, the landing shows a
   broken/empty figure.)
2. **Voice & name** — tell Claude Code:
   > "Rename the guide character from Seba to <YourName> everywhere it appears in
   > the UI and in seba-story-api.mjs, and rewrite its personality to be <describe
   > the voice you want>."

   (The guide's dialogue is generated by `seba-story-api.mjs`, so renaming it there
   changes what the character says.)

## Always after a change
Bump the `?v=YYYYMMDD` version on any `<script>`/`<link>`/asset you edited (so
browsers fetch the new version), restart `node server.js`, and click through it in
the browser. If a change won't show, clear site data (DevTools → Application →
Clear site data) — the service worker caches hard.
